Correct a certificate

The information on a certificate is that which was correct at the time of the event. Information will not be changed to show current information—such as parents’ ages and occupations.

To change incorrect or missing information from the time the event was registered you need to:

  • pre-fill our form
  • download and print the completed form
  • submit it by post or in person with your certified supporting documents.

If you are unable to return the original certificate for replacement and require a new certificate you will also need to complete a certificate application form.

When you can use this form

You can use this form to:

  • correct spelling errors on a certificate
  • change incorrect information supplied at the time of the event
  • add certain missing information from the time of the event—such as occupation details.

This form cannot be used for:

  • changes to information that have occurred since the event—such as a change to a parent’s occupation
  • historical events
  • changes to a name
  • adding the father’s/parent’s details to a birth certificate
  • providing cause of death information—this can only be changed by the doctor (or Coroner) who signed the original medical certificate.

Rules—when correcting or changing a certificate

We will not accept your form if it is not completed in full.

If you need to make changes to the form after you have printed it, you must start again (pre-filling and downloading a new form). We will not accept forms with crossing out, white out or correction fluid/tape on them.

We may contact you for more information and you may need to complete or provide more documents. A fee may also apply.

Supporting documents

You will need to attach documents to support your application.

You need to include a copy of the certificate that you want to correct. If you cannot include a copy of the certificate, you will need to include certified proof of ID.

You should also include the following documents. To correct:

  • the date of birth on a birth certificate—provide an original letter from the hospital where the birth occurred
  • the spelling of a parent’s name on a certificate—provide a certified copy of the parent’s birth certificate showing the correct name
  • marriage details on a birth or death certificate—provide a certified copy of the marriage certificate showing the correct details.

How to apply

To correct or change a certificate:

  1. Read and understand the rules.
  2. Complete, download and print the form.
  3. Get your supporting documents certified by a Justice of the Peace or another authorised person.
  4. Submit your form, the certificate you want changed, and certified documents to us by post or at the counter.

By post

Post your signed form with a copy of the certificate you want replaced and your certified supporting documents to:

Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages
PO Box 15188, City East Qld 4002.

In person

Visit the Brisbane registry counter at 110 George Street, Brisbane with your completed, printed form, the original certificate you want replaced and your certified supporting documents.

Pay the fee

If an error has been made by us, a new certificate will be issued free of charge—only if the original certificate containing the error is returned to us.

If incorrect or incomplete information was given to us by the person providing the details at the time of registering the event, a fee may apply and a new certificate will be issued free of charge—only if the original certificate containing the error is returned to us.

If you are unsure if a fee is payable, we will review the event and request a fee to be paid—only if required.

You can pay:

  • by credit card (Visa or MasterCard)
  • with cash at the counter
  • by cheque in the mail
  • by money order at the counter or in the mail.

Make cheques and money orders payable to the ‘Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages’.
