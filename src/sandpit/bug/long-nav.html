Kirra Beach Restoration Project

The first two stages of the Kirra Beach Restoration Project have now been completed and community consultation for Stage 3 is about to commence.

In February 2009 the Premier of Queensland announced up to $1.5 million funding towards the restoration of Kirra Beach. Since that time, the Department of Environment and Resource Management (DERM) has been responsible for the project management of the Kirra Beach Restoration Project. The Gold Coast City Council (GCCC) is a key stakeholder and the primary service provider for the works.

The Kirra Beach Restoration Project consists of the following three stages:

  • Stage 1: Works were completed in July 2009, with 20,000 cubic meters of sand excavated to fill low-lying beach lagoons at Kirra and for emergency repair works at Palm Beach.
  • Stage 2: Works were completed in December 2009 and November 2010, with approximately 200,000 cubic meters of sand excavated from the Kirra Beach shoreline and used to restore a sand dune buffer at the back of the beach.
  • Stage 3: To investigate sustainable long-term options for managing sand supply to the southern Gold Coast beaches including Kirra Beach.

What did the stages 1 and 2 works achieve?

On completion of stages 1 and 2 of the Kirra Beach Restoration Works (end of November 2010), the beach adjacent to the Kirra Surf Life Saving Club was at its narrowest since May 2003. The GCCC will continue to maintain the site for a further 12 months to ensure the restoration is a success. This maintenance will include weeding, site monitoring, and watering using recycled water. Following this time the GCCC will continue to maintain these dunes as part of a city wide dune maintenance program.

Where to from here?

Following completion of Stages 1 and 2 of the Kirra Beach Restoration Project, DERM is seeking feedback from the community about additional long-term options for further investigation to be included as part of Stage 3.  To provide comments on the Kirra Beach Restoration Project, please visit DERM's website.
