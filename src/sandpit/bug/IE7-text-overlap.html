News

  1. Aquatic Fun Days

    Posted
    10 September 2012

    Aquatic Participation Fun Days offer people with a disability the opportunity to enjoy free, safe and fun water activities. There are also information sessions to help school teachers, learn-to swim-instructors and others in the disability sector learn to support swimmers with disability.

  2. Your Life Your Choice

    Posted
    5 September 2012

    A new initiative has been announced to give people with a disability and their families and carers more choice and control of the supports and services they use.

  3. New Autism Spectrum Disorder resource

    Posted
    30 August 2012

    A new online resource has been launched to support families, early childhood teachers and early intervention service providers to transition children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) into Queensland schools.

  4. Education, training and employment

    Posted
    1 June 2012

    Disability Online has information about employment support for people with a disability, as well as advice about related topics including finding the right job, your rights at work and support and tips for employers and colleagues.

  5. Insight into autism

    Posted
    27 April 2012

    Autism and autism spectrum disorders (ASD) have become more prevalent in Australia over recent years. In fact, over 350 Queenslanders are diagnosed with autism every year. Disability Online explores the world of autism to give you an insight into this condition.

  6. 4RPH—a radio reading service for people with a print disability

    Posted
    17 April 2012

    People with a print disability can tune into RPH—a radio reading service that offers a spoken word alternative to print and online media, such as daily local, state and national newspapers and magazines.

  7. Audio described theatre, ballet and opera

    Posted
    30 March 2012

    Queenslanders who are blind or have low vision can enjoy the highlights of the theatre, ballet and opera season, with an audio description service offered for upcoming performances.
