Prospective juror questionnaire

Complete this form if you have received a Notice to prospective juror letter in the mail.

If you do not fill out this form truthfully—before the date listed on your Notice to prospective juror—you will be guilty of an offence and we may fine you $1,000 or imprison you for 2 months.

It takes around 5 minutes to complete this form.

  1. Your details
  2. Eligibility
    1. Are you any of the following?
      • current member of parliament
      • current mayor or other councillor of a local government
      • current or former judge or magistrate in Queensland or elsewhere
      • lawyer actually engaged in legal work
      • current or former police officer in Queensland or elsewhere
      • current or former corrective services officer or detention centre employee
      • current member of the defence forces
      • unable to read or write English
      • incapable of performing the duties of a juror because of a physical or mental disability
      • 70 years of age or over and do not want to serve on a jury
      • current or former presiding member of the Land and Resources Tribunal
      *
    2. Past offences (If you chose 'No' above)
      1. Have you ever been sentenced to imprisonment or been convicted of an indictable offence? * Drink driving and minor traffic offences are not indictable offences. You should only answer yes about these types of offences if you were imprisoned. If you have been imprisoned, or convicted of an indictable offence, you may not be eligible for some juries.
      2. Details of offence (If you chose ‘Yes’ above)
    3. Select all that apply to you. * (If you chose 'Yes' above)

    4. Not eligible

      • Current members of parliament are not eligble.
      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘current member of parliament’ above)

      • You must still submit this form.

      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘current or former judge or magistrate in Queensland or elsewhere’ above)

      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘current or former correctional officer' above)

    10. Not eligible

      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘current member of the defence forces’ above)

    11. Not eligible

      • People unable to read or write english are not eligible.
      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘unable to read or write english’ above)

    12. Not eligible

      • People incapable of performing the duties of a juror because of a physical or mental disability are not eligible.
      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘incapable of performing the duties of a juror because of a physical or mental disability’ above)

    13. Not eligible

      • People 70 years of age or over and do not want to serve on a jury are not eligible.
      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘70 years of age or over and do not want to serve on a jury’ above)

    14. Not eligible

      • Current or former presiding members of the Land and Resources Tribunal are not eligible.
      • You must still submit this form.
      (If you chose ‘current or former presiding member of the Land and Resources Tribunal’ above)
    15. Medical certificate (If you chose 'incapable of performing the duties of a juror because of a physical or mental disability' above) *
  3. Availability
    1. Are you available to be on a jury for the nominated period on your paper notice? *
    2. Not available – application form for excusal (If you chose ‘No’ above)
      2. Employer statement or medical certificate If you want to be excused from jury duty because of work commitments, or medical reasons, you must have a statement from your employer or a medical certificate.
        • View courthouse contact details.

      If you apply for excusal and we do not accept your reason, we will not contact you to let you know. We will place your name into a second ballot for jury duty.

    3. Have you discussed the possibility of being a juror with your employer? * (If you chose ‘Yes’ above)

    4. Discuss this with your employer

      We recommend you discuss the possibility of being a juror with your employer.
