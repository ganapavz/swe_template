Queensland Education Trust

Queensland is on the cusp of a mining boom; the likes of which we have never seen before.

There is no doubt the coming resource revolution will bring incredible benefits to Queensland—but these benefits must belong to all of us, and most importantly our children.

Queensland’s challenge is to harness the boom to work for all of us.  The Queensland Government wants to find ways to ensure that the wealth in the ground becomes a wealth of opportunity for our children.

We believe that investing in the education of our children is the best way to create a stronger, smarter and fairer Queensland for all of us.  That is why we have already delivered significant reforms to improve education in Queensland—introducing a Prep year, investing $850 million in our State Schools of Tomorrow building program, creating thousands more places in accredited kindy programs, nation-leading learning or earning reforms and now moving Year 7 to High School.  The next step is to take this reform agenda to create new opportunities in higher education and in training.

That is why we are releasing the proposal paper ‘From Mines to Minds: A Queensland Education Trust’.  The Queensland Government will channel royalties from the burgeoning coal seam gas industry to create a Trust that will benefit future generations.  This commitment will be in addition to the billions we already spend on training and education in Queensland.

The proposal paper seeks your views on the best way to establish and manage the Trust to deliver improved educational outcomes in the future. This consultation will remain open until 17 February 2012.
