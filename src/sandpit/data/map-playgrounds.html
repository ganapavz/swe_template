Playgrounds

Outdoor play provides children with the opportunity to explore their environment, have adventures and practice social and physical skills. Playgrounds are a great way for children to get outdoors to play and learn.

  • All abilities playgrounds
  • Local playgrounds
  • Wheelchair swings.

All abilities playgrounds

All abilities playgrounds enable children of all ages and abilities to experience a range of natural and built environments to encourage different types of play.

There are now 17 all abilities playgrounds in Queensland. To find your closest one, view the playground profiles.

Local playgrounds

Many local councils are making their playgrounds more accessible to children with disability. To find out if a council playground in your area suits your needs, contact your local council or view its website. The local government directory includes contact details and web addresses for every Queensland council.

Wheelchair swings

Many playgrounds throughout Queensland have a Liberty Swing that enables a child in a wheelchair or with a physical impairment to experience the exhilaration of swinging through the air.

Download a list of Liberty swing locations (PDF 15KB) (RTF 116KB) with details of where to collect a key to access the swing. You can request a copy of the key from the relevant local council or Variety Queensland so that you can access any swing any time Australia wide.

Variety Liberty Swing Program

A number of these wheelchair swings have been installed with the support of the Variety Liberty Swing Program. If you believe your community would benefit from a Liberty Swing, contact Variety Queensland to discuss funding arrangements and the application process.

Variety Queensland has one portable wheelchair swing that is available for temporary use at events or functions. There is no cost to use the swing. Contact Variety Queensland on (07) 3907 9300 for more information.

Why play is important

Playgrounds offer children many opportunities to play, which is vital for cognitive, emotional, physical and social development. Read the Office for Early Childhood Education and Care fact sheet on why play is important (PDF 421KB).

All Abilities ePlayground

Children of all abilities can experience the joy of play from anywhere through ePlayground—a series of online games. Games can be played a variety of ways, including puffing into a microphone, interactive whiteboard and using the space bar.
