Sport

How can I get involved in social sport?

If you know what sport you are interested in, you can:

  • contact a relevant state organisation for information about programs and services that may be offered in your area. QSport, the peak body for organised and affiliated sport in Queensland, has a list of member sporting organisations that represent different sporting activities in Queensland.
  • use the Australian Government's Play for life club finder to search for a sports club near you, and contact them to find out more about programs they offer.

Who can I contact about disability sport and recreation?

The following organisations specialise in providing sport and recreation services for people with a disability in Queensland:

  • Sporting Wheelies and Disabled Association (Sporting Wheelies) provides information about sports, recreation and fitness programs for people with a range of disabilities. You can call Sporting Wheelies on (07) 3253 3333, email mailbox@sportingwheelies.org.au or visit the Sporting Wheelies website, which includes contact details for regional offices.
  • Life Stream Foundation provides opportunities for people with an intellectual disability to participate in sport and recreation. You can call Life Stream Foundation's Brisbane office on (07) 3394 4399. Contact details for regional offices are available on the Life Stream Foundation website.
  • Special Olympics Queensland supports children and adults with an intellectual disability, providing a range of training and competitive programs for various team and individual sports. Call (07) 3420 5761.
  • Deaf Sports and Recreation Queensland (DSRQ) is the peak sports and recreation body in Queensland for athletes who are Deaf or have a hearing impairment. You can contact DSRQ on dsrq@dsrq.org.au or visit the DSRQ website for more information about their services.
  • The Australian Paralympic Committee (APC) can advise participants about sport and link them in with local clubs. Contact Andrea Johnstone, Development Coordinator for Queensland, on andrea.johnstone@paralympic.org.au or call (07) 3551 1700. For more information, visit the APC website.
  • Riding for the Disabled (RDA) provides sporting and therapeutic equestrian programs for people with a disability and volunteers in centres across Queensland. Information about programs that are offered, and how you can get involved, is available on the RDA website. You can contact the RDA on (07) 3888 5277 or email admin@rdaq.org.au.
  • Sailability Queensland provides sailing opportunities for people with a disability. Contact details for clubs around the state are available on the Sailability Queensland website.

You can also consider:

  • using the Australian Sports Commission disability sport agencies guide to find organisations that facilitate or provide services to support people with a range of disabilities with participating in sport
  • contacting disability organisations to find out about sports programs may be available to meet your needs.

Did you know?

Sports CONNECT, an Australian Government initiative, encourages people with a disability to get involved in sport by developing relationships between sports and disability organisations. You can join a Sports CONNECT HUB near you if you are interested in participating in sport and structured physical activities in a safe, inclusive and fun environment.
