Turning 18?

If your child has impaired decision-making capabilities, you may want a formal decision-maker to be appointed for them when they turn 18 years old.

You can apply to Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) for this when your child is 17 years and 6 months old. The appointment of a Guardian and/or Administrator can commence on your child’s 18th birthday.

Why this is important

Once your child turns 18, banks, for example, may not let you deal with your child’s personal bank account unless you are a legally appointed Administrator.
