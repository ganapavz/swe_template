Legal services

Seniors Legal and Support Service

If you feel you are a victim of elder abuse or that you have been mistreated or taken advantage of financially, help is available. The Seniors Legal and Support Service (SLASS) provides free legal advice, information and social work services for people over the age of 60.

The service can assist you with:

  • legal information and advice
  • social work services
  • short-term counselling
  • advocacy on your behalf
  • a referral to other legal, consumer and support services
  • representation in court or before tribunals (in certain circumstances).

Some examples of how they can help you:

  • obtaining domestic violence protection orders
  • developing safety plans
  • support you to relocate to a place of safety
  • advice on appointing or revoking a power of attorney
  • information on protective strategies to manage your assets and prevent or stop financial abuse
  • advocacy and negotiation on your behalf if you are a resident of a retirement village experiencing unfair contract terms and conditions
  • advising on recovery of property or money following elder abuse or financial exploitation.

You can get help by phone, appointment at a service centre or they can visit you in your home or another safe location.

Seniors’ Advocacy, Information and Legal Service (SAILS)

Provides free legal advice, information and social work services for people over the age of 60.

Contact SAILS on (07) 3214 6333 for further information.

Legal Aid

Legal Aid Queensland can provide you with free legal information, advice and representation if you meet their means and merit test guidelines. They specialise in criminal, family and civil law. Read more about who can get legal aid.

Legal Aid Queensland also provides free legal help for war veterans through the War Veterans’ Legal Aid Scheme.

Contact Legal Aid Queensland for further information.
