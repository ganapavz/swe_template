Applying for a Seniors Card

Card types and benefits

Queensland Seniors Card

This card offers the most discounts.

  • Queensland Government concessions
  • Access to the free Senior Shopper service
  • Discounts on goods and services at participating outlets throughout Queensland

Seniors Card +go

This card offers the same concessions and discounts as above, but also functions as a go card.

  • Queensland Government concessions
  • Access to the free Senior Shopper service
  • Discounts on goods and services at participating outlets throughout Queensland
  • go card for use on public transport in South East Queensland

If you already have a Seniors Card, you can upgrade to the Seniors Card +go and you won't have to pay the $5 refundable card deposit.

Seniors Business Discount Card

If you’re not eligible for the Queensland Seniors Card or Seniors Card +go, you may still be eligible for the Seniors Business Discount Card, which offers discounts on goods and services at participating business outlets throughout Queensland.

Eligibility

Queensland Seniors Card and Seniors Card +go

Residency requirements:

  • You must be a permanent resident of Queensland.
  • You must live in Queensland for at least 6 months of the year and your home address must be shown on your driver licence or be registered as your home address with (at least) 1 of the following:
    • Electoral Commission of Queensland
    • Centrelink
    • Commonwealth Department of Veterans' Affairs

Age/work requirements:

  • You are 65 years or older and not working full-time*
    OR
  • You are 60–64 years, not working full-time* and the holder of one of the below:
    • Pensioner Concession Card
    • Health Care Card
    • Commonwealth Seniors Health Card
    • Department of Veterans’ Affairs Gold, White or Orange card

*Not working full time means working less than 35 hours each week on average in paid employment.

Seniors Business Discount Card

Residency requirements:

  • You must be a permanent resident of Queensland.
  • You must live in Queensland for at least 6 months of the year and your home address must be shown on your driver licence or be registered as your home address with (at least) 1 of the following:
    • Electoral Commission of Queensland
    • Centrelink
    • Commonwealth Department of Veterans' Affairs

Age/work requirements:

  • You are 60 years or older

How to apply

There is one application process for all three card types. You will be issued with the card that offers the most benefits based on your eligibility.

If you have a card but your eligibility has changed, you can update, replace or cancel your card rather than applying again.

You can apply:

Online

Fill out the online seniors application form.

By post

Print and complete the application form, then post it to the address listed on the form:

  • Senior card application (PDF, 860KB)

By phone

Call 13 QGOV (13 74 68) to complete your application over the phone.

Over the counter

Visit a QGAP office or Queensland Government Service Counter.

Proof of ID

You can provide one of the reference numbers below:

  • your Centrelink customer reference number (CRN)
  • your Department of Veterans' Affairs file number

OR

You can provide a photo, scanned copy or photocopy of your:

  • birth certificate
  • passport
  • Queensland driver licence
  • adult proof-of-age-card.

The name on your identity document must be the same name used in your application.

If you do not have any suitable identity documents, you can provide a statutory declaration signed by a Justice of the Peace or Commissioner for Declarations stating your name and address and your date and place of birth:

  • Statutory declaration form (PDF, 11KB)
