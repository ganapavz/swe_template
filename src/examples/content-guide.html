Registering a business name

Who needs to register a business name?

It is an offence under the Business Names Act 1962 to use an unregistered business name or a name that is different from your given or company name. If you do so, you risk being prosecuted or fined. Queensland businesses register their name with the Office of Fair Trading.

Registering your business is important because it prevents you from mistakenly using the name of another Queensland registered business or Australian company. It also lets the public know who is conducting a business under your name, and allows you to open a business bank account under your registered name.

If you are using your own name - your given name/s and/or initial/s followed by your surname - as a business name, it does not have to be registered.

You need to register your business name if:

  • you include other words with your name, such as Joan Smith Party Hire or John Smith & Sons
  • you are trading under a name that is different to your given name
  • you are operating a company (Pty Ltd) and want to trade under a different name to your company name.

Company name or business name?

A company name is different to a business name. While a business name only applies in the state you registered it, a company name can be used in any state in Australia.

If you choose to register a business name, you will have to register it in each state you operate in. If you register a company name, you don't have to register it in each state, unless you want to trade under a different name. A company name is useful if you plan to trade in multiple states.

Registering a company name protects it from being used by competitor. If you want to operate your business as a company, you will need to register with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC).
