Tips

Our online historical search index is free and makes tracing your ancestry simple. Birth, death and marriage records are only available online and you can’t view these records at the registry office. These records are available on microfiche at some libraries, but they have not been produced in this format since 2009.

Getting ready to start

First, you should collect as much information as you can about the person or event you’re searching for. Check whether the event you’re searching for occurred during the years we keep records for (i.e. the access period). If the event occurred outside of this period, you won’t be able to access the record until the appropriate release date
Also, check whether the event occurred in Queensland. If you’re not sure, check other Australian BDM records
Note: Records on the index are based on information supplied to us, so we are not able to ensure their accuracy.

Searching the index

When you search the index for births, deaths or marriages:
  • use last or first names
  • add the date of the event, which may be the exact date (e.g. 01/01/1901), the month and year (e.g. 01/1901) or just the year (e.g. 1901)
  • complete all fields marked with a “*”

Refine your search

Refine your search by adding:
  • first names
  • father’s and mother’s first names
  • mother’s last name
  • end year (when searching a range of dates)
  • year of registration.

Search tips

If you’re having trouble finding your event:
  • consider whether the person was known by another name (e.g. a person who was known by a nickname but was registered under a formal name on documentation)
  • check corresponding documentation (e.g. if you’re looking for a person’s marriage, their death certificate might provide clues)
  • remember that marriage last name searches are not bride/groom specific. Searches using ‘person 1’ last name will return records of either the bride or groom with that last name. For example, if you search for a bride with the maiden name Smith, just enter Smith into the ‘person 1’ last name field. Your results will show a list of brides and grooms with the last name Smith within the specified date range. You can then click on the column headings to sort your results or narrow your search by entering any combination of person 1’s first names, or person 2’s last name and first names
  • end year (when searching a range of dates)
  • ensure you enter at least one date parameter for your search in dd/mm/yyyy format. This must be:
    • the exact date of the birth, death or marriage
    • the month and year
    • just the year
    • a date range using a ‘date to search from’

Using wildcard searches

You can use a wildcard asterisk symbol ‘*’ to search a wider range of records.


If you want to search for a person you think was called Smith but you’re not sure of the spelling, just enter ‘sm*’ (and the appropriate date).


Your search will retrieve every last name starting with the letters ‘sm’, such as Smith, Smallbone, Smiley, Parr-Smith, Smyth, Smythe, Smart and Smerdon.


Enter the wildcard asterisk after any letters (e.g. sm* not before *sm).


You can also use the wildcard in the ‘first names’ and ‘father’s’ and ‘mother’s’ name fields; however, you can’t use the wildcard for any of the ‘date’ fields.


A broad wildcard search may return too many records, resulting in a ‘system timeout’ and an error message asking you to refine your search. For example, a wildcard last name search of ‘S*’ will return every last name starting with S over your requested period.


Sorting your results

To find specific records easier, sort your results by clicking on the column headings. Clicking on the column headings will rearrange the results by highest to lowest or lowest to highest (A to Z or Z to A) for that column.
You can also sort registration numbers to display results from highest to lowest or lowest to highest (e.g.1/1901–1234/1901 or 1234/1901–1/1901).

Index registration number prefixes and codes

Your search results will give you a registration number in the left-hand column.
This number is made up of the year of registration, an index prefix letter or letters (see table below) and the event registration number.
For example, registration number 1901/B2376 indicates that the event was registered in 1901, occurred in Brisbane and was the 2376th registration recorded in Brisbane that year.
Some records may not display an index prefix. Additionally, index prefixes have changed significantly over time, particularly in South East Queensland.
A person born in the Brisbane suburb of Windsor in 1957 would have a registration prefix of ‘B’, indicating a Brisbane birth. However, as Windsor didn’t become part of the Greater Brisbane metropolitan area until 1925, a person born in Windsor during 1880 would have a prefix of ‘C’, denoting a Country birth.

Certificate access policy

We restrict access to certain records to prevent fraudulent use of life event information. You can search online only for life event records outside a restricted access period, as specified in the Queensland Government’s ‘Certificate Access Policy’. If you try to search for records inside the restricted access period, you’ll receive an error message asking you to amend the dates.

Problems downloading images

If you have problems downloading your image, you may wish to try one of the following:

Hint 1

Your PDF-reader software (such as Adobe® Acrobat® Reader) may be too old to open the image. Try installing a more recent version of the software and downloading the image again.

Hint 2

Firewalls and high security levels on your computer can prevent you downloading the image. Check and adjust your browser settings and try downloading the image again.

Hint 3

Use a computer in another place, such as a friend's, a public library or an internet café. Download the image and then print it, or save it to a portable device.

Hint 4

Give a friend or family member the BDM website address, your receipt number and email address and ask them to download the image for you.

Problems reading images

Each image is a scan of the original, often handwritten, register. We’ve made every effort to capture the best possible image of each register; however, age will dictate the condition and legibility of some records.
You can enlarge an image on your screen with this Adobe® Acrobat® Reader tool located in the toolbar at the top of the Acrobat page.
Click the ‘+’ or ‘-‘ buttons to change the size of your Adobe document.
