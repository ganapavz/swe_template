A disability parking permit allows a person with a mobility disability to use designated parking bays that are located close to the entrances of shopping centres, hospitals and other public places. Permits are also available for organisations that transport people with a mobility disability.

Your local council should be able to tell you where disability parking spaces are located in your area as well as any particular parking privileges or concessions that apply.

There are two levels of parking permit (blue and red) which are issued according to your level of mobility impairment and whether your disability is permanent or temporary.

Permits are issued subject to you meeting certain criteria and it costs $13.65 to apply for a permit.
